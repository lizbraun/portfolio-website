DMD Hub

Web Design | UI/UX Design

DMD Hub home page

DMD Hub was made over the course of the semester by a group of 6 Digital Media and Design students for the UConn Web Design Practicum class. The site utilizes HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and Javascript and was made to help DMD students in course selection and overall awareness within the DMD program. UConn Athletics Champions Hall

Motion Graphics | Photoshop

Men's basketball video example

Women's basketball video example

I worked in a team to design 30 motion graphics and mockups of the 2021-2022 UConn Men's and Women's Basketball Teams. These graphics were to be displayed on two-story tall LED boards in the Werth Family UConn Basketball Champions Center. The videos for each respective team have the same layout and format, but the photos and information, such as name and hometown, are adapted for each player. The examples above show the slight differences between the men's and women's videos.

My team and I communicated weekly with UConn clients on zoom or in person and presented mockups and received any feedback. Throughout this project, I embraced teamwork and gained excellent communication skills with my team members and clients. This experience was in collaboration with UConn senior DMD class Agency.

FRAME Lab Update

Web Design

FRAME Lab home page

During my time at UConn, I was hired by professor Na Zhang to update her UConn Human Development and Family Sciences research lab website on Wordpress. Throughout the process I created many wireframes and high fidelity mockups and presented them to Zhang through Zoom. I took her feedback and applied them in my designs and once we were both content with the style, I created the home page and then applied the style to the remaining pages.

The website is hosted on Wordpress and I used both their templates and my own code to style the website. After completing the site, I presented it and also showed Zhang how to add any new studies she completes in the future. This was a wonderful experience, as I was able to advance my skills in Wordpress and work with Zhang who granted me with creative freedom but also had insightful feedback for her website.

CTDA Social Justice Collection

Graphic Design | Logo Design

CTDA Social Justice Collection home page

The Connecicut Digital Archive is part of the Digital Preservation Repository Program at the University of Connecticut. They serve the entire state and are dedicated to the maintenance, delivery, and preservation of a wide range of digital resources for educational and cultural institutions and State Agencies in Connecticut.

For this project, the CTDA wanted to create thematic collections to highlight important content that was sometimes hidden or more difficult to find within the repository. I was tasked with designing a logo for the project and then stylizing the thematic images in a compelling way.

When making the logo, the CTDA team and I talked about how we wanted to amplify certain collections, such as social justice and woman's suffrage. I wanted this idea to translate in the logo, so I designed different variations primarily with a spotlight or sign. We then wanted the thematic collections to be called "featured topics" so that we could include more content, but decided that a spotlight was a good way to combine both ideas. Using colors from the CTDA style guide, I built out many versions, before deciding on the final layout. As a team, we then collaborated on the final details, including colors, layout, and font size. The final logo below will be used for each feature topic collection and the word(s) in the spotlight we coincide with each collection.

About

My name is Liz Braun, a designer that ...

Through my academic and professional experiences I have gained many skills in communication, teamwork, collaboration, and presenting my own work and ideas. I strongly value others' opinions and love receiving and utilizing feedback for my designs. My work and studies have primarily focused on web and UI/UX design, but I also have a background in motion graphics, graphic design, app design, and fine arts such as digital art, painting, and drawing.

Some other random interests I have are musical theater, certain video games, dogs, baseball (the Yankees in particular), and the beach.

I received a B.A. from the University of Connecticut in Digital Media & Design with a concentration in Web/Interactive Media Design. I also completed a minor in Communications.
